Risk Modelling Activities

During the module, I engaged in risk modelling activities, particularly focusing on Monte Carlo simulations as outlined in Winston's guide. This hands-on exercise enhanced my understanding of quantitative risk modelling and its application in predicting potential outcomes and their probabilities.

Questions and Findings:

  • Q: What is Monte Carlo Simulation?

    A: Monte Carlo Simulation is a mathematical technique that allows for the modelling of complex systems by running multiple simulations with varying inputs to predict probable outcomes.

  • Q: How can Monte Carlo Simulation be applied in risk management?

    A: It helps in quantifying risks by providing a range of possible outcomes and their likelihoods, thus enabling better decision-making in uncertain conditions.

Wiki Activity - Security Frameworks

For the Wiki activity, I reviewed articles by Barafort et al. (2018) and Kirvan (2021) and evaluated various security frameworks.

Questions and Findings:

  • Q: Which security framework would you recommend for an international bank?

    A: For an international bank, the ISO/IEC 27001 framework is highly recommended due to its comprehensive approach to information security management systems (ISMS) and its global recognition.

  • Q: Which security framework would be applicable to a large hospital?

    A: For a large hospital, the Health Information Trust Alliance (HITRUST) CSF is suitable as it integrates healthcare-specific regulations and standards, including HIPAA.

  • Q: What about a large food manufacturing factory?

    A: For a large food manufacturing factory, the Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) combined with ISO/IEC 27001 can provide a robust framework for ensuring both food safety and information security.

  • Q: What recommendations would you make to help these organizations comply with the frameworks?

    A:

    • Conduct a thorough risk assessment to identify vulnerabilities.
    • Implement robust access controls and regular security audits.
    • Ensure continuous monitoring and incident response planning.
    • Provide regular training and awareness programs for employees.

Engaging in these activities not only broadened my understanding of different security frameworks but also improved my ability to evaluate and recommend appropriate measures for various organizational contexts.
