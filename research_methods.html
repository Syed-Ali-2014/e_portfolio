Unit 3: Methodology and Research Methods

Seminar: Peer Review Activity
Date and Time: Thursday 15th August 2024 (16:00 BST)

In this unit, I explored different methodologies and research methods used to collect, analyze, and interpret data, with a particular focus on exploratory and conclusive research design.

Key Concepts Covered:

  • Understanding research design and its importance in answering research questions.
  • Exploratory research: A method used to investigate undefined or unclear problems.
  • Conclusive research: A method used to provide specific and actionable results based on clearly defined objectives.
  • Primary and secondary research methods for data collection.
  • Overview of qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods research.

Research Methods Covered:

  • Qualitative Research: Focused on gathering data about experiences, behaviors, and emotions.
  • Quantitative Research: Aimed at analyzing numerical data to discover patterns and make generalizations.
  • Mixed Methods: Integrating both qualitative and quantitative research for a more holistic approach.

On completion of this unit, I was able to:

  • Understand the different research methods (qualitative, quantitative, and mixed).
  • Identify which data collection methods align with each type of research.
  • Determine which research method would best suit my area of investigation.

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

Seminar: Case Study on Privacy

In this unit, I explored various qualitative research methods such as case studies, focus groups, and observations, often used in data collection.

Key Concepts Covered:

  • Case Studies: In-depth research and study of individuals or groups. Case studies help generate hypotheses but are not ideal for generalizing findings due to sample limitations.
  • Focus Groups: Small groups are selected to discuss specific topics, helping to gain insights on the “why” behind behaviors and opinions.
  • Quantitative Observations: Observing numerical values and applying statistical methods to analyze the data.
  • Qualitative Observations: Monitoring and observing characteristics in natural settings, useful for understanding behaviors and attitudes.

On completion of this unit, I was able to:

  • Understand how to carry out case studies, focus groups, and different types of observation.
  • Discuss the advantages and drawbacks of each method.
  • Determine the type of data each method would yield and how it could be applied to my research.

Unit 5: Interviews, Survey Methods, and Questionnaire Design

In this unit, I explored interview techniques, survey methods, and questionnaire design, focusing on the differences between surveys and questionnaires as well as pre- and post-testing methods.

Key Concepts Covered:

  • Interviews: A qualitative research method used to gather in-depth details from respondents through conversation-based questioning.
  • Surveys: A quantitative method that uses structured questions to gather data about opinions, trends, and other measurable information from a large sample.
  • Questionnaires: A set of questions used in both qualitative and quantitative research for gathering information, often part of a survey but can also be used independently.
  • Pre-testing and Post-testing: Techniques used before and after implementing a new process or system to evaluate its effectiveness.

On completion of this unit, I was able to:

  • Understand the difference between good and poor questionnaire design.
  • Learn how to gather responses effectively for my investigation.
  • Analyze the data obtained from surveys and interviews.

  • Learning about measures of location and spread, which help in understanding the central tendency and variability in data.

On completion of this unit, I am now able to:
