Network Security

Network security, including security management and
protection issues and strategies.

Lessons learnt and key outcomes

By the units mentioned below, I learnt to understand the fundamentals of network security and critically plan and carry out vulnerability assessments and penetration testing, as well as learn from case studies to enhance the network security of web applications. By leveraging threat modeling frameworks, such as STRIDE and DREAD, I learnt to respectively identify security vulnerabilities and rank them based on their level of severity or impact. Furthermore, I learnt to appreciate the importance of understanding data privacy regulations, including GDPR and MDR for health-related data, and leveraged them to provide evidence-based informed recommendations to improve network security by ensuring the compliance to such directives when relevant.

Unit 1: History of Network Security, Vulnerabilities and Approaches

During this first unit, I explored the historical development of network security and how it relates to modern security practices. My learning journey encompassed a comprehensive study of the historical evolution of network security, analysis of vulnerabilities, and modern mitigation approaches. Below are the key areas of focus and the insights I gained:

  • Historical Evolution: Studied key historical events in network security and their influence on modern practices.
  • Understanding Vulnerabilities: Explored various types of vulnerabilities, their origins, and impacts.
  • Best Practices in Security: Examined best practices such as layered security and regular patch management.
  • Modern Scanning Techniques: Engaged in practical activities to scan websites for vulnerabilities.
  • Collaborative Discussions: Participated in discussions on the digitalization and security implications.

Unit 2: Advanced Persistent Threats: Applying the Cyber Kill Chain Model to a Case Study

In this unit, I revisited the Cyber Kill Chain model and applied it to the SolarWinds exploit case study. This exercise helped me evaluate the model's relevance in understanding and mitigating modern APTs. The key activities and insights include:

  • Understanding APTs: Discussed the impact and characteristics of Advanced Persistent Threats.
  • Applying the Cyber Kill Chain Model: Analyzed the SolarWinds exploit using the Cyber Kill Chain framework.
  • Case Study Evaluation: Assessed the effectiveness of the model in explaining and mitigating the exploit.
  • Collaborative Discussions: Continued discussions on the digital economy and security implications.
