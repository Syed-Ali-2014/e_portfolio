Case Study: Reviewing an Assessment Reporting Template

Completion Requirements: You are tasked with reviewing the assessment template from PurpleSec.

Instructions

  1. Does this template meet the NCSC stated requirement of preparing a baseline to use as a reference point for pen tests? If not, what changes/amendments would you make?
  2. What are the two best lessons/examples presented in the report?
  3. What two things do you think are unnecessary or could be done more effectively?

Analysis and Responses

1. Does this template meet the NCSC stated requirement of preparing a baseline to use as a reference point for pen tests? If not, what changes/amendments would you make?

Answer: Yes, the template generally meets the NCSC's requirement of preparing a baseline for pen tests. It includes a detailed methodology, scan results, and a risk assessment, which are crucial for establishing a baseline. However, some amendments could enhance its effectiveness:

  • Include a section explicitly stating the baseline configuration and patch levels of the systems before the vulnerability assessment.
  • Add a summary table of vulnerabilities by host to provide a clear overview of the security posture across the network.

2. What are the two best lessons/examples presented in the report?

Answer:

  • Critical Severity Vulnerability Analysis: The detailed breakdown of critical vulnerabilities, including specific plugin names, descriptions, and solutions, provides clear and actionable insights for remediation.
  • Recommendations Section: The recommendations are practical and prioritize immediate actions (e.g., applying patches), which helps in mitigating the most critical risks promptly.

3. What two things do you think are unnecessary or could be done more effectively?

Answer:

  • Excessive Detail in Low Severity Vulnerabilities: The report includes detailed descriptions for low severity vulnerabilities that may not be immediately actionable. Summarizing these could make the report more concise.
  • Redundancy in Scan Results: The report includes both detailed scan results and a Nessus report. Consolidating this information into a single section could streamline the document.

Reflection

1. Did you have any issues or challenges with this activity?

Answer: One challenge was ensuring that the recommendations were practical and feasible for implementation. Additionally, interpreting the detailed technical findings required careful analysis to provide meaningful suggestions.

2. How did you overcome them?

Answer: I overcame these challenges by cross-referencing the findings with industry best practices and consulting additional resources to ensure the recommendations were relevant and actionable. Discussions with peers also helped in validating the interpretations and recommendations.

3. How will they affect your final report?

Answer: These experiences will enhance the final report by ensuring that the recommendations are both practical and effective. The thorough analysis and cross-referencing will contribute to a well-rounded and credible assessment.
