Unit 8: Hypothesis Testing

In this unit, I performed hypothesis testing using Excel. Below are the detailed steps, hypothesis tests, and conclusions based on the provided data.

Step 1: Stating the Hypotheses

  • **Null Hypothesis (H₀):** The population mean income for males is less than or equal to that of females.
  • **Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):** The population mean income for males exceeds that of females.

Step 2: Setting the Criteria for Decision

I set the level of significance (α) at 0.05 for this hypothesis test. This means I will reject the null hypothesis if the p-value is less than 0.05.

Step 3: Computing the Test Statistic

I calculated the t-statistic and p-value using a two-sample t-test with unequal variances. The t-statistic tells us how far the sample mean is from the population mean, taking into account standard deviation and sample size.

Hypothesis t-Statistic p-Value Conclusion
Test of Income for Males vs Females 3.270 0.00071 Reject H₀
Test for Difference in Effectiveness of Diet A vs Diet B 3.072 0.0028 Reject H₀
Test for Preference Between Brand A and Brand B 2.875 0.018 Reject H₀

Step 4: Decision Making

Based on the calculated p-values, I rejected the null hypothesis for all tests. In each case, the p-value was less than 0.05, meaning there is strong evidence that the alternative hypothesis is true.

Findings:
  • The hypothesis test for male vs female income showed that males earn significantly more than females, with a p-value of 0.00071.
  • There was a statistically significant difference in weight loss between Diet A and Diet B, as indicated by a p-value of 0.0028.
  • Brand A was significantly preferred over Brand B, as shown by a p-value of 0.018.
